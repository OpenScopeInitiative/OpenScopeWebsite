About OpenScope

OpenScope is a student-run science communication platform dedicated to making scientific knowledge accessible, engaging, and student-driven.

Our Mission

At OpenScope, our mission is to make science accessible, engaging, and student-driven. We believe that young voices can powerfully bridge the gap between complex scientific ideas and the wider world. Through clear writing, creative storytelling, and community collaboration, we aim to inspire curiosity and cultivate scientific literacy, one article at a time.

Our History

OpenScope began as a simple idea: What if students could explain the science they were passionate about? Founded in April 2025, OpenScope started as a passion project by a high school student recognizing the need for accessible science communication. What began with a handful of articles quickly grew into a collaborative platform featuring contributors across disciplines, backgrounds, and locations.

Founder’s Note

When I first launched OpenScope, I didn’t know what it would become. I just knew I wanted to create something that invited people into science. Too often, scientific knowledge is hidden behind jargon or formal training. But students aren’t just passive learners; we’re curious thinkers, writers, and explorers. This platform is more than a publication. It’s a community of writers and readers, all brought together by the idea that science can be shared.

— Shubhangi M, Founder & Director, OpenScope

Our Values

The principles that guide everything we do

Accessibility

We believe science communication should be available to everyone, regardless of background or prior knowledge.

Clarity

We explain complex scientific concepts in simple, jargon-free language that anyone can understand.

Community

We foster a supportive student community where members can learn, share, and grow together.

Innovation

We continuously explore new ways to make science learning and communication engaging and effective.
